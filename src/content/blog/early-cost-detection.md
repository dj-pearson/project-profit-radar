## The Construction Cost Overrun Timeline Problem

### How Traditional Cost Tracking Works:

```
Day 1: Work performed
Day 7: Time sheets collected
Day 14: Entered into accounting
Day 30: Materials invoiced
Day 45: Project completes
Day 60: All costs received
Day 90: Books closed, actual costs known

90 days from problem to knowledge
```

**By day 90, you can't fix anything. The money is gone.**

---

### The Critical Window

There's a window of time when problems are:
1. **Detectable** (costs are trending wrong)
2. **Fixable** (project still in progress)
3. **Recoverable** (can prevent loss)

**For most projects, this window is Weeks 2-6.**

**Traditional tracking:** Discover problems Week 8-12 (after window closes)
**Predictive tracking:** Discover problems Week 2-3 (while window is open)

**The difference is $40,000+ per year for typical contractor.**

## The 7 Cost Overruns You Can't See Coming (But Should)

### 1. **The Labor Efficiency Creep**

**What it looks like:**
```
Week 1: Crew productive, 8 hours = 8 hours of work
Week 2: 8 hours = 7.5 hours of work (slight drop)
Week 3: 8 hours = 7 hours of work (continuing decline)
Week 4: 8 hours = 6.5 hours of work (significant problem)
```

**What causes it:**
- Site access issues (parking, locked gates)
- Material availability (waiting for deliveries)
- Crew dynamics (personality conflicts)
- Scope confusion (waiting for client decisions)
- Hidden complexity (discovered conditions)

**Traditional tracking:** Discover at project completion
**Predictive tracking:** Alert in Week 2

**Real Example:**
Atlantic Builders, bathroom remodel:
- Alert Week 2: "Labor hours 15% above similar projects"
- Investigation revealed: Crew spending 45 min/day dealing with parking
- Solution: Negotiated driveway parking with neighbor
- Result: Efficiency returned to normal, saved $2,200

---

### 2. **The Material Cost Surprise**

**What it looks like:**
```
Budget: $8,500 in materials
Week 1: $2,100 (on track)
Week 2: $4,800 (slightly high)
Week 3: $7,200 (uh oh)
Week 4: $10,100 (disaster)
```

**What causes it:**
- Price increases (market changes)
- Spec changes (client upgrades)
- Waste/damage (not tracked)
- Quantity estimation errors (bid was wrong)

**Traditional tracking:** Discover when final invoices arrive (Week 8+)
**Predictive tracking:** Alert in Week 2 when trending 12% high

**Real Example:**
Thompson Construction, deck build:
- Alert Week 2: "Material costs 18% over budget"
- Investigation: Lumber prices increased 22% since bid
- Action: Presented cost increase to client with documentation
- Client approved price adjustment: +$2,400
- Result: Protected margin instead of absorbing loss

### **1. Real-Time Cost Capture**

### **2. Continuous Comparison**

System compares in real-time:
- **Budget vs Actual**: Are we over/under budget?
- **This Project vs Similar Projects**: Are costs typical?
- **This Week vs Last Week**: Is trend improving or declining?
- **Expected vs Actual Pace**: Are we burning budget too fast?

**Not just "are we over budget" but "are we TRENDING toward overrun"**

### **3. Early Warning Calculations**

System projects forward:

```

- At current burn rate: $50,900 final cost

```

**Catches problems 2-4 weeks before they become disasters.**

---

### **4. Intelligent Alerts**

Not every variance triggers an alert (that would be noise).

**System alerts when:**
- Cost variance >10% on critical line items
- Labor efficiency <85% of comparable projects
- Material costs trending >15% over budget
- Overall project trending to <10% margin
- Subcontractor invoices >20% over quote

**Smart thresholds = actionable alerts, not noise.**

---

### **5. Recommended Actions**

Each alert includes context and suggestions:

```
Alert: Labor costs 18% over budget
Context: Comparable kitchen remodels average 247 hours,

  1. Review crew schedule for inefficiencies

  3. Consider bringing in additional crew to accelerate completion
  4. Discuss time impact with client if delays on their side
```

**Not just "there's a problem" but "here's what to do about it."**
